Painting for the Brain

Pieter Adriaans

Whoever reads this book will never look at a painting the same way again.
"A painting is a program for the viewing machine in our brain", that is the central proposition from which Pieter Adriaans, painter and professor emiritus of computer science at the UvA in Amsterdam, develops a new vision of painting. Our brain constructs an image of reality based on limited information provided by our eyes. The painter uses it. Recent insights from neurobiology, computer science and mathematics teach us to look in a new way. A book for anyone who is interested in painting as a viewer, maker, teacher, critic, amateur or professional

.

'This book contains the discoveries that you will find in almost no other work on the visual arts. It is not an introduction to drawing and painting. Nor is it a manual for perspective drawing, anatomy, or paint mixing. There are thousands of books that do that better. It is a report of a search; one of the most fascinating adventures I have undertaken in my life' Pieter Adriaans
